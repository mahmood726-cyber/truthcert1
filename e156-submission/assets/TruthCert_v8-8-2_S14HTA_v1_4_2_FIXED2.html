📋 S14.0 Purpose, Design Principles & Fātiḥah Alignment

TruthCert-HTA+ extends TruthCert's clinical inference into policy-safe economic decision support, while preventing harm from false certainty.

S14.0a Design Principles

S14-HTA+ is an executable, pre-specified HTA operations system (HTA-OPS) that:

  • Consumes TruthCert outputs (effect + uncertainty + verdict + Threat Ledger) without altering clinical inference
  • Applies certainty-gated economic inference (Tier A/B/C/D) so HTA cannot claim more precision than TruthCert allows
  • Outputs a structured HTA Certificate, reproducibility bundle, and ledgers
  • Prioritises implementation realism and decision safety: guardrails, managed entry, regret reporting
  • Favors minimum defensible complexity (model ladder) and explicit uncertainty taxonomy (S14.20)
  • Adds safe extensions only when they reduce arbitrariness, strengthen provenance, or prevent implausible outputs

S14.0b Fātiḥah Alignment (Normative)

S14-HTA+ follows the "straight path" of decision-making: it uses the simplest defensible model, refuses precision when evidence is unstable, prioritises preventing harm from false certainty, and requires every assumption to be explicit, logged, and auditable. Economic outputs are gated by TruthCert certainty and cannot exceed what the evidence can bear.

S14.0c Refusal Rules (Hard Constraints)

  • If TruthCert verdict is UNCERTAIN, S14 must refuse precision: no definitive ICER, no adoption recommendation, no PSA; only conservative envelopes + managed entry + "what would change decision"
  • All monetary outputs must be reported in LCU for the selected country pack
  • All stochastic components must be deterministic with recorded seeds
  • S14 may not create new clinical subgroups, infer new treatment effects, or override TruthCert
  • S14 may downgrade actions (e.g., pilot-only), but can never upgrade certainty beyond TruthCert

S14 uses TruthCert outputs as inputs and does not modify them.

🪜 S14.4 Model Ladder & Straight-Path Constraints

S14.4a Model Ladder (Default Order)

1 Decision Tree Acute/short horizon
2 Cohort Markov Model Chronic/recurring
3 DES Capacity Module Queues — conditional
4 Transmission/OneHealth Conditional
S14.4b Primary Selection Rule: Choose the lowest rung that matches disease course + horizon + decision question.
S14.4c Straight-Path Stopping Rule (Hard Constraint):
If Tier C or Tier D, stop at the first defensible rung and do not proceed to DES/Transmission/OneHealth unless required to prevent material harm.
Material harm criterion must be pre-specified in intervention pack; otherwise forbidden and logged ND_TIER_CD_STRAIGHT_PATH_STOP.
S14.4d Last Resort Clause (Hard Constraint — NEW in v1.4):
If simpler model rungs (A–B) lead to the same policy recommendation as more complex rungs (C–D), the higher rungs must not be reported in the primary output.
  • Enforces minimum necessary complexity
  • Complex rungs may be retained in technical appendix only
  • Logged as ND_LAST_RESORT_SUPPRESSED_HIGHER_RUNG
"If the same destination is reached by the simpler path, the complex path must not be shown."

📋 S14.32 Plain Decision Path (Reporting-Only — Mandatory)

No new analysis • No degrees of freedom • Distilled explanation only

Every S14-HTA+ output must include a 5-line Plain Decision Path that summarises the decision in human-readable form. This disarms complexity criticism by providing immediate transparency.

1 Evidence Certainty [TruthCert Verdict: STABLE | MODERATE | EXPOSED | UNCERTAIN]
2 Model Rung Used [A: Decision Tree | B: Markov | C: DES | D: Transmission]
3 Main Driver of Benefit [Parameter/assumption that most favours the intervention]
4 Main Driver of Uncertainty [Parameter/assumption that most threatens the conclusion]
5 Safe Action Category [adopt | adopt_with_guardrails | pilot_only | delay_research | reject]
S14.32b Constraint: The Plain Decision Path is reporting-only and cannot introduce new analysis, subgroups, or claims beyond what TruthCert and S14 have already computed. It must be derivable solely from existing outputs.
S14.32c Hard Requirement: Omission of Plain Decision Path → ND_PLAIN_DECISION_PATH_MISSING → HTA Certificate invalid until remediated.
This 5-line summary must appear in both the HTA Certificate and any policy brief derived from it.
